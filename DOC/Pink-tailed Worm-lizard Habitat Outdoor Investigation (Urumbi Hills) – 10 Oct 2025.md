# Pink-tailed Worm-lizard Habitat Outdoor Investigation (Urumbi Hills) – 10 Oct 2025

1. Objectives

Confirm vegetation and environmental features to prepare for the next phase (focus: distribution and cover of native grasses and forbs; micro-topography and exposed rock surfaces)
Confirm specific habitat/land features visible in aerial (UAV) imagery and complete ground–imagery alignment and annotation (GCPs/scale/reference objects)
Further refine the habitat quality assessment metrics; consult with the client and obtain recommended survey locations
Focal survey area: Urumbi Hills (field plots centered on client-recommended sites and representative outcrops)



2. Schedule

09:30 Depart from ANU hive (ANU Sports) for Urumbi Hills (depart after equipment checks and route confirmation)
On-site ground data collection and UAV verification at target locations (adjustments as needed by weather and field conditions; approximate schedule only)
2.5h on site
Return to ANU by around 13:00 ; complete data backup and a brief daily summary



3. Data to Collect
3.1 Vegetation and Environmental Features

Record species of native grasses and forbs and estimate percent cover
Micro-topographic parameters (slope, aspect, elevation), rock cover and exposure
Presence and distribution of invasive/exotic species (if any)

3.2 Aerial Imagery Alignment and Annotation

Place ground reference items (scale bars, color cards, signage)
Collect high-precision GPS coordinates (plots/GCPs)
Record correspondence between UAV imagery (preferably centimetre-level resolution; multispectral if available) and ground photos

3.3 Habitat Quality Metric Validation

Field-test existing indicators (e.g., rock cover, vegetation composition, micro-topography)
Record notes on indicator operability, robustness, and subjectivity
Gather characteristics and accessibility information for client-recommended locations



4. Equipment List

GPS units (high-precision handheld, at least 2)（maybe）
Measuring tools: tape, rangefinder, clinometer, 1 m × 1 m quadrat frames20*20
Camera gear: cameras, lenses, tripods, standard color cards, scale bars/signage
UAV and accessories: ≥4 spare batteries, SD cards, in-vehicle charger


5. Contingency Plan

Weather: suspend UAV flights during thunderstorms/high winds; switch to ground surveys; add rest and hydration for high temperatures
Equipment: prepare spares for critical gear; use substitutes if needed (e.g., phone GPS, high-quality phone photography)
Personnel safety: first aid for minor injuries; call 000 immediately for serious injuries and contact the team lead; move heat-affected persons to shade and rehydrate
Data safety: back up key data every 2 hours; dual backups (cloud + local); perform on-site completeness checks
Communications: radios + phones; check in with base hourly; predefine emergency assembly point(s)


6. Data Quality Control

Standardize recording formats and coding; establish a quality checklist
Repeat verification for key measurements and define outlier handling procedures
Record ground–imagery alignment and calibration (including GCPs and capture parameters)


7. Follow-up Plan

Same-day data organization and preliminary analysis; data cleaning and validation within one week
Compile vegetation/environmental features and build a sample library for imagery alignment and annotation
Confirm habitat quality metrics and a recommended site list with the client
Outputs: small atlas of feature distributions, ground–imagery correspondence examples, proposed quality indicator list
